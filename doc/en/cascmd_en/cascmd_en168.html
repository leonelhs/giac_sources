6.1.4  Decomposition into prime factors

The ifactor command factors an integer into its prime factors. (Note that a prime factor of a Gaussian integer is only determined up to a factor of ±1 or ±i.)

  • ifactor takes n, an integer or a list of integers.
  • ifactor(n) returns n in factored form (or a list of the integers in factored form).

Examples

ifactor(90)
     
5· 2· 32           
ifactor(-90)
     
−5· 2· 32           
ifactor(14+23*i)
