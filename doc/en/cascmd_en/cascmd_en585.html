15.7.10  Finding linear recurrences

The reverse_rsolve command finds a linear recurrence relation given the first few terms.

  • reverse_rsolve takes v=[v0,…,v2n−1], a vector made of the first 2n terms of a sequence (vn) which is supposed to verify a linear recurrence relation xnvn+k+⋯+x0vk=0 of degree smaller than n, where the xj are n+1 unknowns.
  • reverse_rsolve(v) returns the list x=[xn,…,x0] (if xn≠ 0 then xn is reduced to 1).

In other words, reverse_rsolve solves the linear system of n equations of the form

  xnvn+k+⋯+x0vk=0,   k=0,1,…,n−1.
