6.4.4  Modulus and argument of a complex number

A complex number z can be written in polar form reiθ, where r is the modulus and θ is the argument. The angle θ is only determined up to a multiple of 2π; there will be a unique value in the interval (−π,π], the value in this interval is called the principal value of the argument.

The abs and arg commands find the modulus and argument of a complex number, respectively (see also Section 7.3.2).

  • abs takes z, a complex number.
  • abs(z) returns the modulus |z|.
  • arg takes z, a complex number.
  • arg(z) returns the principal value of the argument of z.
